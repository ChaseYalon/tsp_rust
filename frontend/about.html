BACKGROUND

The Traveling salsmen problem was first proposed by Merrill M. Flood (1) in 1930. The basic idea of the problem is as follows. Imagine you are a bus driver with 20 stuents to drop off. For the sake of example each student has their own stop. Now find the shortest route starting and ending at the school that hits each stop exactly once. This is the traveling salsmen problem. Now taking that example from earlier, the most ovbious idea is to simply try every option and find the shortest one. Now this will work by here is the problem. PRACTICAL APPLICATION

The traveling salsmen problem has many practical applactions. Half are direct, meaning a solution or even a signifagant improvemnt to the traveling salsmen probelm would bring about great change. Some example are package routing, Protien Folding and many more. There are also numerous indirect applications due to a propertey called NP-Completeness. The basics being that if you can prove you have solved the traveling salsmen problem that proof can be used to solve a whole range of problems. This is perhaps the most important application of the travling salsmen probelm.
